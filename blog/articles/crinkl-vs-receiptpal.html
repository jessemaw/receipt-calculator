How Each Receipt Scanning App Works

How Crinkl Works

Crinkl is a newer receipt rewards app that pays in Bitcoin through Cash App. The base rate is approximately $0.10 per receipt, but the actual payout varies based on what you purchased. Grocery and restaurant receipts can earn up to $0.30 with category multipliers, while other categories earn $0.15-$0.20.

The key advantage of Crinkl is no weekly receipt limits. While ReceiptPal caps you at 12 receipts per week, Crinkl lets you scan as many as you have. For households with frequent shopping trips, this makes a significant difference in monthly earnings.

Crinkl also offers seasonal leaderboards where top scanners earn bonus rewards, and brand missions that pay extra for purchasing specific products. Points never expire, which is a major advantage for casual users who might not scan every week.

How ReceiptPal Works

ReceiptPal has been around since 2015, making it one of the more established receipt scanning apps. The earning structure is straightforward: scan receipts, earn points, redeem for gift cards. The math works out to approximately $0.057 per receipt (about 11-12 points per receipt, with 200 points equaling $1).

ReceiptPal's standout feature is e-receipt integration. You can connect your email account, and the app automatically scans digital receipts from online purchases. This is entirely passive — you earn points without lifting a finger for your Amazon, Walmart, and other online orders.

The trade-off is ReceiptPal's strict limits. You can only scan 12 receipts per week (maximum 3 per day). Points also expire after 90 days of inactivity, so you need to stay engaged with the app. On the positive side, ReceiptPal has a long track record of reliable payouts and offers familiar redemption options like Amazon and Visa gift cards.

E-Receipts: Where ReceiptPal Shines
